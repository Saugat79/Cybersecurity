# OSI Layer 1 – Physical Layer

## Introduction

The Physical Layer is the first layer of the OSI model.  
It is responsible for transmitting raw data bits over a physical medium such as cables or wireless signals.
- Physical layer do converts the data into bits.
This layer deals with hardware components and signal transmission.

---

## Main Functions of the Physical Layer

- Transmitting raw bits (0s and 1s)
- Defining electrical and physical specifications
- Signal transmission over media
- Data encoding and signaling
- Physical connection between devices

---

## Principles of the Physical Layer

- Data is transmitted in the form of signals
- Signals can be electrical, optical, or radio waves
- Physical devices and cables are required for communication

---

## Signals

### Analog Signals
- Continuous signals
- Represent information using varying waves
- Example: Radio signals

### Digital Signals
- Discrete signals represented by 0s and 1s
- Used in computer networks

---

## Physical Components

Examples of physical layer components include:

- Network cables
- Repeaters
- Network interface cards (NIC)
- Connectors
- Hubs

---

## Transmission Media

### Wired Media

Wired communication uses physical cables to transmit data.

#### Ethernet Cable (Twisted Pair)
- Most common network cable
- Used in LAN networks
- Example: Cat5e, Cat6

#### Fiber Optic Cable
- Uses light to transmit data
- Very high speed
- Used for long-distance communication

---

### Wireless Media

Wireless communication transmits signals using electromagnetic waves.

Examples include:
- Wi-Fi
- Bluetooth
- Radio signals
