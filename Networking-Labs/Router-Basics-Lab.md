## Objective
To understand the basic function of a router and how it connects different networks.

---

## What is a Router?

A router is a networking device that connects multiple networks together and forwards data packets between them using IP addresses.

Unlike a switch, a router operates at Layer 3 (Network Layer) of the OSI model.

---

## Devices Used

- 1 Router
- 1 Switches each side
- 3 PCs each side
- Ethernet cables

---

## What I Learned

- Router connects different networks.
- Router works at Layer 3.
- Router uses IP addresses to forward packets.
- Without router, two different networks cannot communicate.

---

## Why Router is Important in Cybersecurity

- Controls traffic between networks
- Can apply Access Control Lists (ACL)
- Helps segment networks
- Important for firewall placement
