## Objective
To understand how a repeater extends the network signal between two switches.

---

## What is a Repeater?

A repeater is a network device that regenerates and amplifies signals so they can travel longer distances in a network.

It operates at Layer 1 (Physical Layer) of the OSI model.

---

## Devices Used

- 1 Repeater
- 2 Switches
- 4 PCs
- Ethernet cables

---

## How the Network Was Built

- Two PCs connected to Switch 1
- Two PCs connected to Switch 2
- Switch 1 connected to the repeater
- Repeater connected to Switch 2

The repeater helps extend the signal between the two switches.

---

## What I Learned

- Repeaters regenerate signals.
- They work at the Physical Layer.
- They extend network distance.
- Repeaters do not filter or control traffic.

---

## Difference Between Repeater and Switch

| Repeater | Switch |
|--------|--------|
| Layer 1 device | Layer 2 device |
| Regenerates signal | Forwards frames |
| No traffic control | Intelligent forwarding |
